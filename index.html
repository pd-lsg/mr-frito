Plantain Fries — Operational Note
Concept Assessment
Plantain fries as a direct replacement for French fries is a genuinely differentiated item with real commercial potential. The Mr. Frito branding commits to this clearly — "Dominican Street Food Elevated" with the green plantain mascot. The operational challenge is significant but manageable with the right prep discipline. This is the calling card. Everything else on the menu exists to support it.
⚠ Core Operational Challenges
Sourcing consistency: Green (unripe) plantains required — they hold structure in the fryer. Ripe plantains break down. Same ripeness level needed every delivery. Requires a reliable wholesale supplier, not a retail run. Prep labor: One box of plantains ≈ 90–120 minutes of prep labor. Dedicated role on any meaningful volume day. Plan accordingly or the kitchen backs up before service even starts. Oxidation: Peeled plantains brown quickly. Must be stored in water immediately after peeling. Means buckets of water in cold storage — volume to be confirmed based on walk-in allocation.
Questions to Resolve with Chef Before Soft Open
1. Can par-cooked plantain fries be frozen and fried to order without quality loss? 2. Fry time from: (a) raw, (b) par-cooked, (c) frozen? 3. Holding time after frying before quality degrades? 4. Optimal cut thickness for even cooking? 5. Oil type and temperature? 6. Seasoning spec: salt only, or a house blend? Consistency requires a written spec. 7. Pre-cut and hold in water for >4 hours? Test for texture impact. 8. Realistic output per fryer basket per minute at service pace?
Decision Required: Plátano Only vs Plátano + French Fries
Recommendation: Plátano fries only. The current menu offers both — "your choice of French fries or Plátano fries" on the Frites section. This needs to be resolved before the menu is printed. (1) Differentiation — offering both dilutes the story and splits fryer capacity. (2) Operational simplicity — one fry product, one prep protocol, one supplier. (3) It signals confidence. The mascot is a plantain. Commit to it. If guest pushback is material after two weeks of trading, revisit. Launch with conviction.
Supplier Recommendations — Green Plantains
Establish primary and backup supplier before ordering anything. — Restaurant Depot (Queens, Bronx) — open to food service businesses, no membership fee. — Hunts Point Produce Market (Bronx) — largest produce wholesale market in the US. Direct importer access. Negotiate volume pricing once you know weekly usage. — Caribbean/Latin wholesale distributors in Queens — consistent green plantain supply given local demand. COD terms for first 90 days — build this into opening cash reserve.
